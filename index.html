Who Am I?

You can currently find me in Boulder, CO; though I call Pittsburgh, PA home. I research data ethics as a PhD student at CU-Boulder, do design and engineering work on technical art installations, and spend free time building, writing, and making art with CoLab. I asbolutely love writing sci-fi and translating computer science ideas to the public.

Recently, I wrote and co-produced a theater and engineering hybrid project that was a winner of the John S. and James L. Knight News Challenge on data. Our show, "Quantifed Self" brought audience members into the center of a sci-fi drama about the ethics of personal data use, where their own shared data personalized the experience. I also take care of a beauitful piece of land in Tennessee with a community of artists and engineers called CoLab, and sometimes do work with Fast Forward Labs, mostly on their Deep Learning report.

This website houses documentation for my projects, some of my writing, and an occassional blog to share thoughts and what I'm up to.
