Who Am I?

Most of my time is spent coding, writing, creating art, and building things. My areas of expertise are data science, democracy, education tech, and visualization. Right now, I am a PhD student at University of Colorado - Boulder in the Computer Science Department working under Tom Yeh. I also do work with Fast Forward Labs, specifically on their Deep Learning report and research. Outside of these affiliations, I am the co-founder of an organization, CoLab, that has varied interests across our members, though generally education technology, art, public science, and sustainable living/design tend to be at the forefront. If you really want to know my passtions, a more exhaustive list of my interests would be: writing (fiction and non-fiction), education and teaching, art, coding, manual labor, music, agriculture, building things, community, law, and democracy.

Current Projects

  1. Quantified Self Art Show
    • I received a gracious grant from University of Colorado's "Engineering Excellence Fund" to put on an art show around our relationship to the data we create
    • The first run of the show will be April 12-17, 2016 in Boulder,CO in the ATLAS Institute's Blackbox Theater
  2. ModuLearner
    • With CoLab, we are working to create a free, modular online textbook that is centered on project-based learning. We also are working on a platform that uses this content to help teachers and learners design curriculum that is centered on project-based approaches
    • If you are interested in learning more or think you'd like to contribute to our text go to our project page.
  3. Students for Instructional Improvement
    • Right now, I am leading a senior project team of computer science undergraduates at CU-Boulder. Our task it to create a student-focused alternative to our university's survey system that we used to collect data on classes each semester
    • Our belief is that we've largely gathered the wrong data about classes, and if students should have a direct voice in shaping what kind of data collect, and also be able to access and use that data easily.
    • We hope to have a version of our site up by the end of 2015
  4. Democracy in the Digital Era
    • Right now I am teaching a new course at Colorado called, "Democracy in the Digital Era" which relates historical and contemporary issues of democracy to their new context of information and digital media.
