Who Am I?

I am a data ethicist, writer, artist, educator, and concerned citizen based out of Pittsburgh, PA. Currently, I am nearing the defense of my PhD thesis at CU-Boulder. However, I am also now the executive director at Community Forge, a mission-driven organization dedicated to rehabbing the Johnston Elementary School in Wilkinsburg, PA into a community and small-business center. Please explore my website, which stays (somewhat) up-to-date with my current projects and writings.

Outside of community-driven work, my intellectual interests involved ethics; particularly data ethics and how to develop technology in the public-interest. Over the coming months, I'm working to expand an arts/education outreach effort that was first piloted with support by the John S. and James L. Knight News Challenge. The immersive theater piece, "Quantifed Self" brought audience members into the center of a sci-fi drama about the ethics of personal data use, where their own data personalized the experience. A passion project I put my free time into involves taking care of a beauitful piece of land in Tennessee with a community of artists and engineers called CoLab. In the past, I've done projects with Fast Forward Labs, the Inclusive Design Institute, and the EQUIP research team.
